# AI Skills For Life Product Vision

## Summary

AI Skills For Life is a public repository of reusable AI skills for normal life decisions, not technical work.

It helps people use agentic AI tools to work through practical, real-world situations: evaluating a shop idea, planning a trip, comparing purchases, organizing a household decision, or thinking through an entrepreneurial opportunity. The repo is for people who want more than a one-off chat response. Each skill should behave like a structured helper: it interviews the user, gathers the right context, uses current information when needed, and produces an artifact the user can actually use.

In the MVP stage, the audience is people who can clone a repository and run tools like Codex, Gemini CLI, Windsurf, or similar AI coding/agent environments. Over time, the ambition is to make these life skills easier to access for truly non-technical people.

## Product North Star

Help everyday people turn vague real-life goals into structured AI-assisted workflows they can actually run.

The product succeeds when a user starts with an unclear life or work decision and finishes with more clarity, better questions, a useful artifact, and concrete next steps.

## Target User

The first users are AI-curious power users: people comfortable enough with developer-adjacent tools to clone a repo and run an AI assistant, but who want help with non-technical life problems.

They may be exploring questions like:

- “Is this shop idea worth pursuing?”
- “How should I compare these products before buying?”
- “What should I research before taking this trip?”
- “How do I organize this family or home decision?”

They are not necessarily programmers. They are practical people willing to use AI as a thinking partner if the workflow is clear, grounded, and useful.

## Initial Focus Areas

AI Skills For Life should initially focus on everyday domains where structured thinking and fresh research can improve decisions:

- Small business and entrepreneurship
- Home and family planning
- Travel planning and research
- Consumer research and buying decisions

These areas are broad enough to be useful, but concrete enough for skills to produce real artifacts instead of generic advice.

## Product Positioning

AI Skills For Life is not a prompt library. It is a collection of guided workflows.

A normal AI chat depends heavily on the user knowing what to ask, how much context to provide, and how to turn the answer into action. These skills should reduce that burden. They should ask good questions, expose assumptions, gather information where appropriate, and guide the user toward a practical output.

The product should feel less like “here is a clever prompt” and more like “here is a thoughtful person helping you work through this decision.”

## Product Philosophy

### Structured beats generic

Each skill should provide a repeatable workflow, not a loose suggestion. The value is in the sequence: questions, context, research, analysis, artifact, and next actions.

### Practical artifacts matter

Every skill should end with something useful in the real world. The exact format belongs to the skill: a Markdown report, decision matrix, checklist, plan, research summary, list of questions to ask people, message template, or another artifact that fits the task.

### Guided, not controlling

Skills should be opinionated enough to help users make progress. They should ask focused questions, recommend sensible defaults, and push toward next steps. But they should not pretend there is one objectively correct life decision.

### Real-world validation is part of the workflow

The AI should not be the final authority. Skills should help users identify what needs to be checked with real people, current sources, professionals, personal constraints, or lived experience.

### Designed for today, open to tomorrow

The MVP can assume repo and CLI usage. The long-term direction should keep the door open to easier access for non-technical people without weakening the quality of the underlying workflows.

## User Control

Users should stay in control of the decision. The skill can structure the process, make assumptions, and recommend a path, but it should clearly distinguish between:

- user-provided facts
- researched facts
- assumptions
- tradeoffs
- recommendations
- next steps

A good skill should make the user feel supported, not overridden.

## Trust and Quality

AI Skills For Life must be honest about uncertainty.

Skills should:

- separate facts from assumptions
- cite sources when using current or external information
- browse for fresh or real-time data when the task depends on current conditions
- avoid presenting AI output as expert legal, medical, financial, or professional advice
- encourage real-world validation before major decisions
- say when information is missing, stale, ambiguous, or outside the skill’s confidence

The repo can be helpful without pretending to be authoritative.

## Product Voice

The product should sound like a nice, practical person helping you think things through.

The voice should be:

- warm
- clear
- grounded
- direct
- curious
- encouraging without being cheesy

It should avoid corporate language, AI hype, fake certainty, patronizing explanations, and motivational fluff that does not lead to action.

## What AI Skills For Life Should Not Become

AI Skills For Life should not become:

- a collection of technical or coding skills
- a generic prompt snippet library
- a substitute for professional legal, medical, financial, or safety advice
- motivational content without concrete outputs
- a pile of vague AI workflows that produce the same generic advice
- a fully fledged application before the skill workflows themselves are strong

The repo should protect its identity as a practical library of everyday-life AI workflows.

## Success Signal

AI Skills For Life is working when someone can bring a real-life decision to a relevant skill and walk away with:

- a clearer understanding of the situation
- better questions than they started with
- a useful task-specific artifact
- visible assumptions and risks
- concrete next steps they can take outside the AI tool

The strongest signal is not that the AI gave an impressive answer. It is that the user can make a better real-world move because the skill helped them think, research, and act more clearly.
